SCO Bordner Land Use Application

This application is a joint project between the State Cartographer's Office and the Mladenoff Lab in Russell Labs on the UW-Madison campus. It serves the purpose of highlighting land use types through unique raster layers while also offering the option of overlaying with vector line and point-based features that help further illustrate the landscape. The user can toggle between a standard web Leaflet tiled basemap, satellite view, and an original 1930's aerial photography basemap layer.

My involvement with this application has been in the form of developing scripts and automated processes that can easily take source photos from the 1930's, mosaic them into townships for georeferencing and finally mosaic into an entire county to be tiled for the purposes of web visualization. All coastal counties have a historical aerial photo basemap layer present with more inland counties being gradually filled in as time allows.
